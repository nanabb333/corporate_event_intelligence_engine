# Sample CEO Departure Analyst Brief

## Question

What usually happens after an abrupt CEO departure?

## Historical Analogs

- Uber (UBER), 2017-06-20: abrupt resignation under investor pressure; pathway: governance repair.
- WeWork (WE), 2019-09-24: founder resignation under board and investor pressure; pathway: governance repair.
- Papa John's (PZZA), 2017-12-21: founder resignation under reputational pressure; pathway: reputational repair.
- Hewlett-Packard (HPQ), 2010-08-06: conduct-related resignation; pathway: governance enforcement.
- Wells Fargo (WFC), 2016-10-12: crisis-linked resignation; pathway: reputational repair.
- McDonald's (MCD), 2019-11-03: conduct-related termination; pathway: conduct accountability.
- Twitter (TWTR), 2021-11-29: founder stepdown; pathway: founder transition.

## Observed Pathways

- governance repair (2 cases): Leadership change is part of repairing governance, culture, controls, or investor confidence.
- reputational repair (2 cases): The company uses leadership transition as one part of restoring public, customer, or stakeholder trust.
- conduct accountability (1 case): The departure centers on policy enforcement and leadership standards.
- founder transition (1 case): Founder leadership gives way to a successor while governance continuity remains a central issue.
- governance enforcement (1 case): The board emphasizes investigation, process, and governance standards after conduct concerns.

## Analyst Notes

- Uber: The case is a core example of abrupt founder exit linked to governance repair, cultural remediation, and board pressure.
- WeWork: The case is a strong analog for founder-led companies where governance, disclosure, and capital-market scrutiny force leadership restructuring.
- Papa John's: The case captures a founder CEO exit where brand reputation and public controversy drove leadership transition before further governance changes followed.
- Hewlett-Packard: The case is an early high-profile example of CEO departure following board investigation, with the observed pathway centered on governance process and interim leadership.
- Wells Fargo: The case shows CEO departure as an accountability signal following a misconduct scandal with continuing governance and control remediation.
- McDonald's: The case is useful for separating personal-conduct termination from operating-performance termination; the pathway centers on governance enforcement and leadership continuity.
- Twitter: The case separates founder departure from crisis departure: the transition emphasized internal succession and governance continuity despite prior external pressure.
